Surface Current

In oceanography, a gyre (/ˈdʒaɪər/) is any large system of circulating ocean currents, particularly those involved with large wind movements. Gyres are caused by the Coriolis effect; planetary vorticity along with horizontal and vertical friction, determine the circulation patterns from the wind stress curl (torque)

Gyres:
Formation

Rotation cause Coriolis effect that deflects the air, causing wind.
Due to the Ekman transport theory, the surface water is driven by the wind.
When adding the eastern and western continent, water piles up forming a high pressure dome.
Pressure of the dome counteract and make the water deflected in 45 degrees.
The pressure drive the water movement and therefore form the 5 major gyres and the circumpolar current.
